### MDR-ANNEX-II-1.1 | EU-MDR | Device description and specification
The technical documentation shall contain a full device description and specification, including the product or trade name, intended purpose, intended users, variants/configurations, accessories, and the principles of operation and mode of action. It shall include a general description, materials, and key functional elements.
SOURCE: Regulation (EU) 2017/745, Annex II §1.1 (EUR-Lex, public)

### MDR-ANNEX-II-1.1c | EU-MDR | Intended purpose, indications and target patient groups
The documentation shall state the intended purpose, the intended patient population, the medical conditions to be diagnosed/treated/monitored, the intended user profile, and the intended use environment, with sufficient specificity to scope the clinical and risk evidence.
SOURCE: Regulation (EU) 2017/745, Annex II §1.1(c) (EUR-Lex, public)

### MDR-ANNEX-II-2 | EU-MDR | Information supplied by the manufacturer (label and IFU)
The documentation shall include the label(s), packaging, and instructions for use in the languages accepted in the Member States where the device is to be sold, covering safe use, residual risks, warnings, contraindications, and the UDI carrier.
SOURCE: Regulation (EU) 2017/745, Annex II §2 (EUR-Lex, public)

### MDR-ANNEX-II-4 | EU-MDR | General safety and performance requirements (GSPR) conformity
The documentation shall contain the information needed to demonstrate conformity with the General Safety and Performance Requirements (Annex I) applicable to the device, including a GSPR checklist identifying each requirement, its applicability, the method used to demonstrate conformity, and the controlled documents that provide evidence.
SOURCE: Regulation (EU) 2017/745, Annex II §4 (EUR-Lex, public)

### MDR-ANNEX-II-5 | EU-MDR | Benefit-risk analysis and risk management
The documentation shall include the benefit-risk analysis and the risk management documentation, demonstrating that risks have been reduced as far as possible and that the residual benefit-risk balance is acceptable, with reference to the risk management plan, analysis, and the report.
SOURCE: Regulation (EU) 2017/745, Annex II §5 (EUR-Lex, public)

### MDR-ANNEX-II-6.1 | EU-MDR | Pre-clinical and clinical data, verification and validation
The documentation shall contain the results and conclusions of verification and validation, including pre-clinical testing (e.g. engineering, bench, electrical safety, EMC, software V&V, biocompatibility) and clinical data, demonstrating the device meets its specifications and intended purpose.
SOURCE: Regulation (EU) 2017/745, Annex II §6.1 (EUR-Lex, public)

### MDR-ANNEX-I-3 | EU-MDR | Risk management system (GSPR 3)
Manufacturers shall establish, implement, document and maintain a risk management system as a continuous iterative process across the device lifecycle, requiring planning, identification of hazards, estimation and evaluation of risks, risk control, and evaluation of the impact of production and post-production information.
SOURCE: Regulation (EU) 2017/745, Annex I §3 (EUR-Lex, public)

### MDR-ANNEX-I-10.1 | EU-MDR | Chemical, physical and biological properties (biocompatibility)
Devices shall be designed and manufactured to ensure compatibility between materials and biological tissues, cells and body fluids, accounting for the intended purpose, and to minimise risks from substances that may leach or off-gas. Biological safety shall be evaluated.
SOURCE: Regulation (EU) 2017/745, Annex I §10.1 (EUR-Lex, public)

### MDR-ANNEX-I-14.2 | EU-MDR | Devices incorporating electronic programmable systems and software
Software shall be developed and manufactured in accordance with the state of the art taking into account the principles of development life cycle, risk management (including information security), verification and validation. Repeatability, reliability and performance in line with intended use shall be ensured.
SOURCE: Regulation (EU) 2017/745, Annex I §14.2/17 (EUR-Lex, public)

### MDR-ANNEX-I-23 | EU-MDR | Label and instructions for use content
The label and instructions for use shall carry the information needed to identify the device and manufacturer, and to use the device safely — including intended purpose, residual risks, contraindications, warnings, precautions, and performance characteristics.
SOURCE: Regulation (EU) 2017/745, Annex I §23 (EUR-Lex, public)

### MDR-ANNEX-III-1 | EU-MDR | Post-market surveillance plan
The manufacturer shall plan, establish, document, implement, maintain and update a post-market surveillance system proportionate to the risk class, including a PMS plan addressing the collection and use of data, indicators, and methods for evaluating collected data.
SOURCE: Regulation (EU) 2017/745, Annex III §1 (EUR-Lex, public)

### MDR-ANNEX-XIV-1 | EU-MDR | Clinical evaluation and clinical evaluation plan
The manufacturer shall plan, conduct and document a clinical evaluation establishing and updating a clinical evaluation plan, including identification of the GSPRs requiring clinical data, the intended clinical benefits, and the methods to appraise clinical data.
SOURCE: Regulation (EU) 2017/745, Annex XIV Part A (EUR-Lex, public)

### ISO-14971-CL5 | EU-MDR | (ISO 14971) Risk analysis

### ISO-14971-CL7 | EU-MDR | (ISO 14971) Risk control measures
