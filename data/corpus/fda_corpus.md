# US FDA 510(k) regulatory corpus (US-FDA jurisdiction)
#
# Copyright note: FDA regulations (21 CFR) and FDA guidance documents are public
# (U.S. government works). ISO standards referenced by FDA (e.g. ISO 10993) are
# copyrighted — only the recognised-consensus-standard intent is paraphrased.

### FDA-510K-SE | US-FDA | Substantial equivalence to a predicate device
A 510(k) must demonstrate that the device is substantially equivalent to a legally marketed predicate device: same intended use, and either the same technological characteristics or different characteristics that do not raise different questions of safety and effectiveness, supported by performance data.
SOURCE: 21 CFR 807.92 / FDA 510(k) Program guidance (public)

### FDA-510K-IFU | US-FDA | Indications for use statement
The submission must include a clear Indications for Use statement describing the disease/condition the device diagnoses, treats, prevents, or monitors, the target population, and the use environment, consistent with the predicate's intended use.
SOURCE: FDA Form 3881 / 510(k) guidance (public)

### FDA-510K-PERF | US-FDA | Performance testing (bench and as applicable clinical)
The submission must include performance testing demonstrating the device meets its specifications and performs comparably to the predicate — including bench testing protocols, acceptance criteria, results, and a rationale for the test selection relative to the device's technological characteristics.
SOURCE: FDA 510(k) performance testing guidance (public)

### FDA-510K-BIO | US-FDA | Biocompatibility evaluation
For patient-contacting devices, the submission must include a biocompatibility evaluation appropriate to the nature and duration of contact, following an FDA-recognised consensus approach (ISO 10993 family) — endpoints addressed, testing or justification, and conclusions.
SOURCE: FDA "Use of ISO 10993-1" guidance (public); ISO 10993 itself is copyrighted (intent only)

### FDA-510K-SW | US-FDA | Software documentation
For devices containing software, the submission must include software documentation commensurate with the documentation level (risk), covering software description, requirements, architecture, V&V testing, and a revision-level history.
SOURCE: FDA "Content of Premarket Submissions for Device Software Functions" guidance (public)

### FDA-510K-CYBER | US-FDA | Cybersecurity
For devices with connectivity or software, the submission must address premarket cybersecurity: a secure product development framework, threat modeling, vulnerability management, and labeling of security controls and residual risks.
SOURCE: FDA premarket cybersecurity guidance (public)

### FDA-510K-LABEL | US-FDA | Labeling
Device labeling must comply with 21 CFR 801 and include adequate directions for use, warnings, precautions, and the indications for use; labeling must not be false or misleading and must support safe and effective use by the intended user.
SOURCE: 21 CFR 801 (public)

### FDA-510K-RISK | US-FDA | Risk management information
The submission should include risk management information identifying hazards, risk controls, and residual risk for the device, supporting the performance testing selection and the overall safety and effectiveness conclusion.
SOURCE: FDA 510(k) guidance / recognised risk management practice (public)
