# VitalTrack TM-200 — Technical Documentation

Class IIa continuous skin-contact body temperature monitor with companion mobile app.

## Device Description and Intended Purpose
The VitalTrack TM-200 is a reusable, battery-powered wearable patch that continuously measures peripheral skin temperature via a calibrated NTC thermistor and transmits readings over Bluetooth Low Energy to the VitalTrack companion application (TM-App v2.3). The system comprises the TM-200 sensor module, a single-use adhesive carrier, an inductive charging cradle, and the TM-App. The principle of operation, signal chain, calibration coefficients, and all hardware/software variants are specified in DOC-DESC-001 rev C. Accessories and configurations are enumerated in DOC-DESC-002.

## Intended Purpose and Indications
The TM-200 is intended for continuous monitoring of peripheral body temperature trends in adults (≥18 years) in general-ward and home settings, as an adjunct to standard clinical assessment. It is not intended for use on neonates, in intensive care, or as the sole basis for diagnosis. The intended user is a healthcare professional or a trained lay user. The intended use environment, target population, and contraindications are specified in DOC-IFU-010 rev B.

## GSPR Conformity
A complete General Safety and Performance Requirements checklist (DOC-GSPR-100 rev D) maps each applicable Annex I requirement to its applicability rationale, the method used to demonstrate conformity, and the controlled evidence document. Non-applicable requirements are justified individually.

## Risk Management
Risk management is conducted per a documented plan (DOC-RM-200) across the device lifecycle. The risk analysis identifies intended use and foreseeable misuse, hazards and hazardous situations, and estimates each risk. Risk control measures are implemented and their effectiveness verified; residual risk and overall residual risk are evaluated and judged acceptable against defined criteria in the Risk Management Report (DOC-RM-205 rev C). A production and post-production information loop feeds field data back into the analysis.

## Clinical Evaluation
A clinical evaluation plan (DOC-CE-300) identifies the GSPRs requiring clinical data, the intended clinical benefits, and the appraisal methodology. The clinical evaluation report (DOC-CE-305 rev B) appraises a systematic literature review and equivalence data, concluding the benefit-risk profile is acceptable for the stated intended purpose.

## Verification and Validation
Verification and validation results are documented in DOC-VV-400. Electrical safety (IEC 60601-1) and EMC (IEC 60601-1-2) testing passed with reports referenced. Temperature accuracy was validated at ±0.2 °C across the 34–42 °C range against a traceable reference. Software verification and system validation against requirements passed with defined, pre-approved acceptance criteria. Shelf-life and adhesive ageing studies are summarised.

## Biocompatibility
The skin-contacting adhesive carrier was evaluated following a recognised consensus approach for limited (<24 h) intact-skin contact. Cytotoxicity, sensitisation, and irritation endpoints were addressed by testing; results and the biological evaluation conclusion are documented in DOC-BIO-500 rev B.

## Software Lifecycle
TM-App firmware and mobile software are developed under a documented software development life cycle with software requirements, architectural design, unit/integration/system testing, and configuration management, appropriate to the software safety classification. Information security and cybersecurity controls are addressed. The software documentation and revision history are in DOC-SW-600 rev C.

## Labeling and Instructions for Use
The label and instructions for use (DOC-IFU-010 rev B) provide device and manufacturer identification, intended purpose, residual risks, warnings, contraindications (neonates, ICU, sole diagnostic use), precautions, and performance characteristics, with the UDI carrier. The IFU is provided in the required Member State languages.

## Post-Market Surveillance
A post-market surveillance plan (DOC-PMS-700 rev B) proportionate to the Class IIa risk defines data sources, indicators, the PSUR cadence, and the methods for evaluating collected data, with a defined link to the risk management and clinical evaluation update processes.
